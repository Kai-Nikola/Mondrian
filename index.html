Piet Mondrian

Piet Mondrian was a Dutch painter born on March 7, 1872, in Amersfoort, Netherlands. and art theorist, a pioneer of abstract art. His work is characterized by simple, geometric shapes like rectangles and lines, primarily in black, white, gray, and primary colors. Mondrian sought to express universal harmony and balance through his art, a philosophy he termed "Neoplasticism." His iconic style influenced countless artists and designers, making him a foundational figure in modern art.

Mondrian's art was highly utopian and was concerned with a search for universal values and aesthetics. He proclaimed in 1914: "Art is higher than reality and has no direct relation to reality. To approach the spiritual in art, one will make as little use as possible of reality, because reality is opposed to the spiritual.

Piet Mondrian was hailed as one of the greatest artists of the 20th century by critic Robert Hughes. Hughes praised Mondrian for his belief in art's transformative power to improve human life. His work, characterized by its radical simplicity and pursuit of universal harmony, has had a profound impact on art and design.
